Contraction

The term ‘contraction’ refers to the shortened form of two words with an apostrophe that marks the missing letter in the contracted form.

Some contractions can have multiple meanings (e.g., "it's" can mean "it is" or "it has").

Type Full Form Contraction Example
Subject + Verb I am I'm I'm going to the store.
Subject + Verb You are You're You're invited to dinner.
Subject + Verb He/She/It is He's/She's/It's She's excited about the party.
Subject + Verb We/They are We're/They're They're arriving tomorrow.
I/You/We/They have I've/You've/We've/They've I've I've finished my homework.
He/She/It has He's/She's/It's He's He's had enough.
Will (all pronouns) Will 'll I'll see you later.
Would (all pronouns) Would 'd I'd like to help.
Verb + Not Is not Isn't The movie isn't very long.
Verb + Not Are not Aren't They aren't coming to the party.
Verb + Not Will not Won't I won't be late for dinner.
Verb + Not Cannot Can't She can't swim very well.
Verb + Not Do not Don't We don't like seafood.
Verb + Not Does not Doesn't It doesn't rain much in the desert.
Verb + Not Did not Didn't They didn't attend the meeting.
Verb + Not Have not Haven't I haven't seen that movie yet.
Verb + Not Has not Hasn't She hasn't finished her homework.
Verb + Verb Would have Would've I would've gone if I had known.
Verb + Verb Could have Could've We could've won the game.
Verb + Verb Should have Should've I should've studied harder.

Words that I learnt from the story

Savouring: Enjoying and appreciating something fully, especially food or a moment.
Sentence: She was savouring every bite of her delicious dessert.

Scooped: Picked up or moved something using a scoop or a similar tool.
Sentence: He scooped some ice cream into a bowl.

Minced: Cut into very small pieces, usually food.
Sentence: She minced the garlic before adding it to the pan.
